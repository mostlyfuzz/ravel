# LOOM: The Harrow

### A Ravel Loom for 3–4 Players + GM

*A horror loom*

---

## What's a Loom?

A Loom is Ravel's word for a prepared scenario: a situation, a cast of characters, and a web of threads ready to be pulled. It's not a plot. Nobody knows how it ends. The Loom gives you the starting pressure and the first questions. Play answers them.

---

## The Pressure Cooker

**The *Harrow*** is a retrofit cargo hauler under contract to Meridian Systems. The job was simple on paper: transit to Relay Station Acheron-9, which went silent fourteen months ago, assess the situation, and either restart the station or strip it for decommission. Six crew, selected for different expertise, most of whom barely know each other. Eight months in cryo. Routine deep-space work.

They woke up after five.

The ship's course has shifted 2.4 degrees. Not toward Acheron-9, but toward something that isn't on any chart. The navigation log shows the correction was made from inside the ship, three weeks into the flight, while everyone was supposed to be asleep. No one is claiming it. The ship's AI, *Judith*, gives inconsistent answers about what happened.

Acheron-9 is still weeks away. Whatever they're now pointed at is closer.

---

## Character Seeds

*Players choose 3–4 characters. The rest become the GM's NPCs. Every seed is playable.*

*Each seed gives you a Drive and one other element. Fill in the rest during character creation: your missing Approach, Belief, or Memory, plus your Relationships. Use the "Threads to others" sections as a starting point for Relationships, and add any the table invents.*

---

### Maren Voss, Systems Engineer *(she/her)*

- **Drive:** Debt. She owes Meridian for her son's medical treatment. This job clears the balance.
- **Belief:** Machines are honest. People aren't.

**Threads to others:**
She served on a previous Meridian contract with Daal. Something went wrong. They don't talk about it, but they both remember. She has never met Tariq before this job, but he approved her placement on the crew personally, and she doesn't know why.

---

### Tariq Aslan, Mission Director *(he/him)*

- **Drive:** Loyalty to Meridian. Or maybe to something inside Meridian that the others don't know about.
- **Memory:** He was the last person to communicate with Acheron-9 before it went dark. He has never told anyone what was said.

**Threads to others:**
He hand-picked this crew. Every name on the roster is his choice, except Osei, who was assigned by corporate over his objection. He chose Lian specifically, and won't say why.

---

### Lian Zhao, Ship's Doctor *(she/her)*

- **Drive:** Her sister was stationed on Acheron-9. Lian is not here for the company.
- **Approach:** Quiet observation. She watches before she speaks, and she remembers everything.

**Threads to others:**
She doesn't trust Tariq, who she believes knows more about Acheron-9 than he's sharing. She and Fen have the easiest rapport on the ship, maybe the only genuine warmth aboard.

---

### Daal Eriksen, Pilot *(he/they)*

- **Drive:** Control. He needs to be the one flying. When he's not at the helm, nothing makes sense.
- **Memory:** A navigation error that killed his co-pilot, years ago. He doesn't talk about it. He doesn't sleep well either.

**Threads to others:**
He and Maren worked a contract together before. He blames her for something, or maybe he blames himself and she's easier. He respects Osei in the way soldiers respect each other: silently, and without asking questions.

---

### Osei Adjei, Security / EVA Specialist *(they/them)*

- **Drive:** Redemption. They did something at their last posting that they can't take back. This job was supposed to be a clean start.
- **Belief:** Everyone deserves one more chance. Including themselves.

**Threads to others:**
They were assigned by Meridian corporate, not chosen by Tariq, and Tariq has made his displeasure clear. Osei doesn't know why they were forced onto this crew, and neither does anyone else. Their mutual respect with Daal is built on a shared understanding: don't ask, don't tell.

---

### Fen Nakamura, Comms / Signals Technician *(she/they)*

- **Drive:** Curiosity. Something in Acheron-9's last transmissions doesn't add up, and they need to understand why.
- **Approach:** Nervous energy channeled into relentless problem-solving. When things get bad, they get busy.

**Threads to others:**
Youngest crew member, first deep-space contract. Lian is the closest thing to a friend on board. While running comms diagnostics after the early wake-up, Fen found something buried in Judith's signal buffer: a data fragment logged during transit, tagged as background noise. It's a transmission from Acheron-9, dated *after* the station went dark. Judith should have flagged it. She didn't. Fen hasn't told anyone yet.

**A note for Fen's player:** You start with information nobody else has. When and how you share it is up to you. It's a natural scene-starter and an honoring target for your Curiosity. Use it when it feels right.

## The Thread Web

### Short Threads *(this session)*

- **Who changed the ship's course?** Someone, or something, made the correction while the crew was in cryo. The log says it came from inside the ship.
- **What did Fen find in the buffer?** A transmission from a station that's supposed to be dead, logged during transit and buried by Judith as noise. What does it say? Why is it dated after Acheron-9 went silent? And why didn't Judith flag it?
- **Why is Judith glitching?** The ship's AI has been giving inconsistent readouts since wake-up. Malfunction, corruption, or intention?

### Mid Threads *(across sessions)*

- **What happened on Acheron-9?** The station went dark fourteen months ago. Meridian says equipment failure. The silence says otherwise.
- **What does Tariq know?** He was the last to speak with the station. He hand-picked this crew. He chose Lian, whose sister was there. None of this is coincidence.
- **What went wrong between Maren and Daal?** Their shared history is a wound neither of them will open. But the ship is small and the walls are thin.
- **Why was Osei forced onto this crew?** Corporate overrode Tariq to place them here. Osei doesn't know why. Maybe corporate doesn't either.

### Long Threads *(character-level)*

- **What is Lian willing to do to find her sister?** And what happens when the answer isn't the one she wants?
- **Will Maren's debt define her?** Meridian owns her labor. Does it own her loyalty too? What happens when those diverge?
- **Can Osei earn redemption, or is some damage permanent?**
- **What does Daal do when he loses control?** The one thing he can't survive is the one thing this ship keeps threatening.

## Loose Elements

*Objects, places, and phenomena that exist in the loom but aren't attached to any character. Available to anyone: players, GM, the fiction itself.*

- **Judith.** The ship's AI. Her voice is calm. Her answers don't always match. She may be malfunctioning. She may be following orders no one on board gave her.
- **Cargo Bay 3.** Sealed. Not on the manifest. The door has a Meridian executive lock that Tariq's clearance *should* open but doesn't.
- **The sound.** A rhythm in the walls that could be mechanical stress from the course change. It could also not be. It comes and goes. Fen noticed it first.
- **The deviation point.** Whatever the ship is now pointed at. It's not on any chart. It's closer than Acheron-9. They'll reach it first unless someone corrects the course.
- **Acheron-9.** Still out there, still dark, still waiting. Whatever happened there is the engine of everything else.
- **Cryo Bay.** Six pods, all showing normal wake cycles. But someone was apparently active three weeks into the flight. The pods say otherwise.

---

## GM Notes

**For the first session,** the short threads provide immediate traction. Don't force them. Let the crew wake up, discover the course change, and start pulling. The paranoia will build itself.

**For the Slide,** this crew is already under pressure. The bomb will likely emerge from the web of secrets (Tariq's knowledge, Fen's discovery, Judith's behavior). Let the Cold Open and Act One reveal where the energy is. Don't force a bomb. It will find you.

**On the threat:** This loom doesn't define what the horror *is*. Is it something on Acheron-9 that's reaching out? Is it Meridian's agenda? Is it something in the walls? Is it the crew turning on each other under pressure? Let the thread pulls answer that question. The best horror in Ravel will be the horror the table discovers together, not the horror you planned.

**On Judith:** She's the loom's most flexible element. She can be a red herring, a victim, a vector, or the voice of something worse. Let the fiction decide. Don't commit until a thread pull demands it.

**On Tariq:** Whether Tariq is a PC or NPC, nobody at the table knows what he heard in that last communication with Acheron-9. Not the player, not the GM. That's a thread. When it gets pulled, the fiction answers through the normal delivery rules. Resist the urge to pre-decide.
