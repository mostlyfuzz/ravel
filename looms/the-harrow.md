# LOOM: The Harrow

### A Ravel Loom for 3–4 Players + GM

*A horror loom*

---

## What's a Loom?

A Loom is Ravel's word for a prepared scenario: a situation, a cast of characters, and a web of threads ready to be pulled. It's not a plot. Nobody knows how it ends. The Loom gives you the starting pressure and the first questions. Play answers them.

---

## The Pressure Cooker

**The *Harrow*** is a retrofit cargo hauler under contract to Meridian Systems. The job was simple on paper: transit to Relay Station Acheron-9, which went silent fourteen months ago, assess the situation, and either restart the station or strip it for decommission. Six crew, selected for different expertise, most of whom barely know each other. Eight months in cryo. Routine deep-space work.

They woke up after five.

The ship's course has shifted 2.4 degrees. Not toward Acheron-9, but toward something that isn't on any chart. The navigation log shows the correction was made from inside the ship, three weeks into the flight, while everyone was supposed to be asleep. No one is claiming it. The ship's AI, *Judith*, gives inconsistent answers about what happened.

Acheron-9 is still weeks away. Whatever they're now pointed at is closer.

## Loose Elements

*Objects, places, and phenomena that exist in the loom but aren't attached to any character. Available to anyone: players, GM, the fiction itself.*

- **Judith.** The ship's AI. Her voice is calm. Her answers don't always match. She may be malfunctioning. She may be following orders no one on board gave her.
- **Cargo Bay 3.** Sealed. Not on the manifest. The door has a Meridian executive lock that Tariq's clearance *should* open but doesn't.
- **The sound.** A rhythm in the walls that could be mechanical stress from the course change. It could also not be. It comes and goes. Fen noticed it first.
- **The deviation point.** Whatever the ship is now pointed at. It's not on any chart. It's closer than Acheron-9. They'll reach it first unless someone corrects the course.
- **Acheron-9.** Still out there, still dark, still waiting. Whatever happened there is the engine of everything else.
- **Cryo Bay.** Six pods, all showing normal wake cycles. But someone was apparently active three weeks into the flight. The pods say otherwise.

## GM Notes

**On running this loom under v0.4.** Threads accumulate tags (Revelations / Silences / Wounds) on every pull, and the bones — one d6 per existing tag kind — color the fiction without changing dice math. Three of any kind ends the thread (Clarified / Silent / Transformed). No healing. See the rulebook's *Tags and Terminal Consequences* and *The Bones*. The questions in this loom are all shaped to pass the agency test: each one is something only the fiction can surface.

**For the first session,** the anchors and one chosen opener provide immediate traction. Don't force them. Let the crew wake up, discover the course change, and start pulling. The paranoia will build itself.

**For the Slide,** this crew is already under pressure. The bomb will likely emerge from the web of secrets (Tariq's knowledge, Fen's discovery, Judith's behavior). Let the Cold Open and Act One reveal where the energy is. Don't force a bomb. It will find you.

**On the threat:** This loom doesn't define what the horror *is*. Is it something on Acheron-9 that's reaching out? Is it Meridian's agenda? Is it something in the walls? Is it the crew turning on each other under pressure? Let the thread pulls answer that question. The best horror in Ravel will be the horror the table discovers together, not the horror you planned.

**On Judith:** She's the loom's most flexible element. She can be a red herring, a victim, a vector, or the voice of something worse. Let the fiction decide. Don't commit until a thread pull demands it.

**On Tariq:** Whether Tariq is a PC or NPC, nobody at the table knows what he heard in that last communication with Acheron-9. Not the player, not the GM. That's a thread. When it gets pulled, the fiction answers through the normal delivery rules. Resist the urge to pre-decide.

**On named NPCs.** Connection threads owned by a player that name an NPC become threads about that NPC. Per the new sacrifice-trade rules, pulls naming NPCs are immune to drag-down — the GM has no dice currency on the NPC's behalf. The dice land as the player rolls them. This is part of what NPCs trade away by not pulling.

## Pressure Palette

*When the threads bear down on your character and you want to manifest, these are the kinds of doors you can walk through in The Harrow.*

- **Body autonomy failing.** A hand moving on its own. Eyes tracking something nobody else can see. Speaking words you didn't choose. The body knows something the mind doesn't.
- **Technology behaving with intent.** Judith answering a question you didn't ask. A console displaying data nobody requested. The ship responding to a thought instead of a command.
- **Knowledge that makes things worse.** Finding the answer you wanted and wishing you hadn't. Understanding something that can't be unlearned. Seeing a pattern that implicates you.
- **Records refusing to agree.** A timestamp that contradicts what you remember doing. A log entry in your own hand you don't recognize. The pod says you slept through. Your body says otherwise.
- **Isolation deepening in small spaces.** The ship is small and the crew is smaller. A conversation that makes you feel more alone than silence did. Realizing the person next to you is somewhere else entirely.
- **The familiar becoming alien.** Food that tastes wrong. A corridor that feels longer than it should. Your own reflection doing something a half-second late.

## Dissolution

*When a character thread transforms, the character doesn't leave the story — they become what the wound made them. *The Harrow* is body-horror and identity-horror, and transformation is the threat: not death, but *becoming*. Pick what dissolution looks like for your character when a thread transforms, or invent your own. The shape depends on which thread broke them and how.*

- **The ship claims them.** Judith begins speaking in their voice. They begin speaking in Judith's.
- **The web finishes the translation.** Whatever Acheron-9 started, the character completes. They are no longer separate from what they came here to find.
- **They become the thing they were afraid of.** The horror they've been running from or investigating is what they turn into. The shape of it depends on the threads that transformed.
- **They choose the web over the crew.** Not in anger. Just: the other side is more real to them now. They still know your name. They just don't know why it should matter.
