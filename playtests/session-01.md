# Playtest Session 1: The Harrow

**Date:** 2026-02-09
**Loom:** The Harrow (horror, Fractures module)
**Players:** 2 (each playing characters + rotating GM)
**Characters in play:** Maren Voss, Lian Zhao, Daal Eriksen
**NPCs:** Tariq Aslan, Osei Adjei, Fen Nakamura

---

## Setup

- Players chose characters from the Harrow seeds
- Blanks on character sheets left intentionally empty to be filled during play
- Maren filled in Approach: Methodical during her first scene
- Convention established: `[brackets]` for meta communication (honoring, thread naming, GM calls)

## What Happened

### Maren's Spotlight (framed by player)

Maren went to the bridge to find Daal running diagnostics on the course deviation. Daal shared what he could see on the instruments. Maren honored Approach: Methodical and earned a die. She pulled on "what do the cryo logs show" with 1 die. Rolled a 1: Breaks. The logs revealed that both Maren's and Daal's pods cycled at the exact timestamp of the course correction. Neither remembers waking up.

- **Dice earned:** 2 (Approach: Methodical, Belief: Machines are honest)
- **Dice spent:** 1
- **Net pool:** 1
- **New thread:** "Were Maren and Daal awake during transit?"
- **Established fiction:** Both pods cycled during the course change

### Daal's Spotlight (framed by player, other player GMing)

Daal alone on the bridge, running the course data for the fourth time. He honored Drive: Control and earned a die. Attempted to engage manual override to correct the course back to Acheron-7. Pulled "should we stay the new course or point back to Acheron-7" with 1 die. Rolled a 4: Cold. Judith locked manual override with a safety protocol and refused to say who authorized the lockout.

- **Dice earned:** 1 (Drive: Control)
- **Dice spent:** 1
- **Net pool:** 0
- **New thread:** "Who controls Judith?"
- **Established fiction:** Manual override is locked. Judith won't explain why.

### Lian's Spotlight (framed by GM)

GM framed: Lian running post-cryo physicals in the med bay. Tariq on the table. Lian spotted a restricted file with Acheron-7 personnel medical records attached to Tariq's file. Her sister's name would be in there. Lian honored Approach: Quiet Observation during the physical exam. Earned a die. Pulled "any clues in the physicals that point to differences in cryo cycles" with 1 die. Rolled a 2: Breaks. Tariq grabbed Lian's wrist, shut down the exam, and on his way out said "Your sister. Jun, right?" He knew Jun's name without being told.

- **Dice earned:** 1 (Approach: Quiet Observation)
- **Dice spent:** 1
- **Net pool:** 0
- **New thread:** "How does Tariq know about Jun?"
- **Established fiction:** Tariq knows Jun's name. He knows Lian is looking for her sister.

### The Tilt

Initial roll (d6/2 round up) gave 3 breaks. This was immediately too much. Even 2 felt like overload. Retconned to 1 break.

**Thread broken:** "Who changed the ship's course?" A second course correction happened live, in front of everyone. Judith did it. Nobody asked. The ship accelerated toward the deviation point. Daal is locked out. Nobody is in control.

Session paused after the Tilt for reflection.

---

## Design Issues Discovered

### 1. Fiction Boundary: Free Detail vs. Thread Pull

**Problem:** During Maren's scene, Daal (played by GM) added that "the authorization code is blank, like it was scrubbed." This is new fiction that narrows the answer to "who changed the course" toward someone with system access and intent to cover tracks. It was added for free, no pull.

**Proposed rule:** You can add detail that opens questions, but detail that narrows answers toward a thread costs a pull. The table can call it, same as honoring. If someone feels a detail is answering a thread rather than setting one up, they speak up and the detail either gets retracted or becomes a pull.

### 2. Tilt Severity

**Problem:** 3 breaks collapsed the fiction. Even 2 felt like too much after a single round of spotlights. The Tilt should create complications, not remove threads from the board.

**Proposed change:** 1 break for early sessions. Scale up as the thread web thickens in later sessions. The Tilt is a crack, not a collapse.

### 3. Player Placement in Opening Scene

**Problem:** GM placed all characters in the opening cryo wake scene. Players should place themselves to respect their agency.

**Proposed change:** In the first session, each player describes where their character is and what they're doing as the scene opens. After session one, players locate themselves in the space before scene construction begins.

### 4. Explicit Thread Naming

**Problem:** During play it was sometimes unclear what constituted a thread vs. general scene activity. Threads felt fuzzy.

**Proposed change:** Name threads explicitly during scene setup so the table knows what's in play. "The threads on the table for this scene are..." Anything can still become a thread during play, but starting with named ones gives focus.

### 5. Pronouns on Character Seeds

**Observation:** Osei uses they/them. Other characters need explicit pronouns. Consider mixed pronoun representation (she/they, he/they).

---

## What Worked

- The honor-to-dice loop felt natural and didn't break immersion
- Token placement (conceptual in this playtest) as silent honoring is better than verbal announcement
- Breaks were genuinely dramatic both times they hit
- The frame-or-pull spotlight tradeoff created real collaborative tension
- Cold on Daal's scene (Judith locking override) was active, interesting fiction
- The Tilt at 1 break created exactly the right amount of pressure
- Contesting didn't come up but the design space for it was clearly present

## Open Questions for Next Session

- How does the second round of spotlights feel post-Tilt?
- Will dice accumulation become an issue as the session progresses?
- Does thread naming at scene start help or feel heavy?
- **Do GM dice matter?** The GM earned zero dice this session, pulled zero threads, and the fiction still moved. Player pulls and the Tilt drove everything. The GM's value was in framing, NPC delivery, and calling scenes. It's possible the GM doesn't need their own dice economy at all, or it may become important once NPCs have richer established elements. Watch for this.
