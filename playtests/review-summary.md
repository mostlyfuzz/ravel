# The Harrow: Playtest Summary (Episodes 1-4)

A condensed review of four episodes playtested with 2 players, 4 characters, and evolving rules. For reviewers evaluating whether the current RULEBOOK.md supports the play it produced.

---

## Setup

**Loom:** The Harrow (sci-fi horror). A crew of six wakes from cryo to find their ship 2.4 degrees off course, pointed at something not on any chart.

**Characters:**
- **Maren Voss** — Systems engineer. Meticulous, increasingly motherly. Belief evolved from "Machines are honest, people aren't" to "Machines are machines, people are people." Carries a false memory of two crew members dying on a prior mission (they survived).
- **Lian Zhao** — Ship's doctor. Searching for her sister Jun, who disappeared when Acheron-9 went dark. Memory: "I never forget" — now a burden as gaps appear. Something has been using her nervous system as an interface since hour 504.
- **Daal Eriksen** — Pilot/engineer. Drive evolved from Control to Understanding. His mother set the ship's course decades before anyone knew the destination existed. He stayed when he could have left.
- **Fen Nakamura** — Comms specialist. Her curiosity was weaponized: she replayed a transmission 43 times thinking she was investigating. She was answering a call. 1 Break.

## What Happened (by episode)

### Episode 1 (pre-episode structure)
Spotlight rotation with a Tilt. Maren discovered both her and Daal's cryo pods cycled during the course change. Daal found the manual override locked. Lian learned Tariq knows her sister's name. The Tilt broke the course thread: Judith made a second correction live.

**Key finding:** The session lacked narrative arc. "Stuff happened." This led to the episode structure redesign.

### Episode 2
First episode structure test. Fen found a transmission from Acheron-9 dated after the station went dark. Lian pulled on who's speaking: Cold. The voice could be Jun. Can't be certain. Daal confronted Tariq: Clarity revealed Jun sent him coordinates and said to bring Lian specifically. Judith screamed when she processed hidden biometric data. Maren found a partition living in Judith's systems: a biological model of alien life, dormant cargo, waiting to arrive.

**Bomb:** "What is Tariq hiding?" **Collision:** Multiple threads detonated simultaneously as Jun's plan was revealed. **Tag:** The biological model zooms out to match Lian's eye color. Jun's data is meant for Lian.

### Episode 3
Lian is in cryo with the partition following her down. Fen's Break: the message was never for Lian, it was bait for Fen's curiosity. Daal found the deviation point: a massive organic web structure in space, grown not built, with thousands of near-human biological signatures 98.6% identical to Lian. Visions triggered by the number sequence 7215 revealed a lineage echo: Maren's son, Daal's mother, Fen's unknown mother, Lian and Jun. Fen completed the 45th response at the collision, her hands moving exactly like her unknown mother's (manifestation). Judith: "It wants to remember through her. It's been forgetting for a very long time."

**Bomb:** "Why did Daal stay?" **Collision:** Fen completes the 45 responses, manifesting. **Tag:** The Harrow stops. Judith goes dark. Lian rises from cryo. The web fills the window. "She's here."

### Episode 4
The Harrow has arrived. Fen recognized the web's geometry matches the transmission's structure. Maren proposed pointing the receivers inward: if the crew are nodes, what's flowing out? Lian dreamed of the shape she'd been reaching toward for three episodes. It grabbed her. Eyes appeared in the back of its head. She thought: *not Jun.* It said: "I am there." (Manifestation.)

**Bomb:** "The transmission is not over." **Bomb scene:** Maren reversed the receivers. Lian's heartbeat was having a conversation with the ship's signal. Fen: "We're keeping time for it." The web spoke through the speakers: "They think they special. Nothing special." Lian and Fen collapsed. **Echo:** Split-scene monologues. Maren and Daal each told someone about a past incident (Meridian-7). Daal's version: Maren saved two people. Maren's version: two people died. Clarity: they survived. Maren's memory is false. **Collision:** Maren's eyes went black, she mouthed the web's words and lunged at Daal's throat. He caught her wrists and held. Clarity on whether Maren comes back: the player chose not yet. **Aftermath:** Judith still unreachable (Cold x2). Daal's mother is alive, she followed, her ship is approaching. The web absorbs what it no longer needs: a shuttle collapsed into it and new connections grew. **Tag:** A message from Daal's mother's ship in a language nobody can read. Maren fled the room like a scared animal. Permission to bridge connect.

## Rules tested and confirmed

**The core loop (honor → dice → pull → outcome).** Worked in every session. Natural, never broke immersion. The economy is tight: earning feels meaningful, spending feels costly.

**Episode structure.** Transformed the game. Episode 1 (pre-structure) lacked arc. Every episode after had narrative coherence. The vocabulary (bomb, echo, collision) orients the table without feeling prescriptive. By the second episode the structure was invisible.

**Cold as weight.** The most important discovery. One thread (Jun won't turn around) went Cold three times across three episodes and became the emotional spine of the campaign. Cold is not failure. Cold is the thread gaining weight. Some threads may never resolve and that is the point.

**Bomb naming.** Emerged naturally at the end of every Act One. Never forced. The table finds its central tension through proliferation, not planning.

**Manifestation.** Produced the strongest moments: Fen's hands at the 45th response, Lian's dream, Maren's attack with black eyes. The rule is light (no dice, no resolution, just show the weight) and that lightness is why it works.

**Fiction boundary rule.** Opening is free, closing costs a pull. Caught and corrected multiple times across sessions. The rule held everywhere.

**Thread branching.** Fractal quality. Parent threads resolve into sharper children. "What does Tariq know?" became five threads. The fiction gets more precise as play continues.

**Breaks as transformation.** Fen's Break in Episode 2 retroactively reframed her entire first episode. Devastating at 11% probability with 2 dice. The rarity is a feature.

**Clarity as door.** Clarity opens more than it closes. Every Clarity branched into new threads. The rule "Clarity is a door, not a wall" is load-bearing.

**Delivery rule.** The person the thread is about narrates the outcome. Tested most powerfully when a player received Clarity on "will Maren come back?" and chose: not yet. The dice say the answer is clear. The player decides what it is.

**Tag as twist.** Every tag planted a fuse. "Two voices. One frequency." "She's here." "Permission to bridge connect." One twist, something is different. Enough structure to work, light enough to surprise.

## Rules refined through play

**Bomb scene** — originally thin ("a thread breaks open"). Refined to: multiple characters present, layers of escalation, the break points at something terrible without naming it.

**Echo scene** — originally required manifestation. Removed: the echo is development, not climax. Manifestation is available, not mandatory.

**GM role** — simplified to baton holder. The GM earned zero dice across all sessions. Value is in framing, noticing what's dormant, calling scene endings. Not an economy participant.

**Tokens** — removed entirely. Physical dice handle the economy. Honoring order: deliver, point, validate.

**Scenes** — simplified. Anyone frames. GM frames when nobody does. Spotlight rotation dropped. Every character in at least one scene per act.

**Thread limit cleanup** — expanded from "drop or absorb" to: keep questions that are interesting, drop what isn't. Retire, collapse, merge, transform. The table decides.

**Offers and Agency** — added as its own section. Framing another player's character is an offer, not a decision. The owner confirms or redirects. A player can always say no.

**Session shape checklist** — rewritten with reflection prompts. After each phase: what changed? Which threads evolved? Where's the energy?

---

## Rules untested

**Contesting pulls.** Never used across four episodes. The table has been collaborative. May emerge in the finale when characters are compromised and pulling against each other.

**Third Break.** Nobody has reached it. The rule (you lose the character to themselves) is untested.

**Full table (4+ players).** All playtesting was 2 players with 4 characters. Character ownership was fluid. A full table with dedicated characters would test agency boundaries, spotlight distribution, and whether dormant NPCs become a problem.

## Key design discoveries

1. **The structure internalizes fast.** By Episode 2, the vocabulary guided decisions invisibly.
2. **The collision comes from character competence.** Maren saved Lian's cryo and opened the bridge. Maren reversed the receivers and discovered the crew is infrastructure. The best collisions come from characters doing what they're good at and the fiction turning it against them.
3. **Cold threads are the emotional spine.** The threads that refuse to resolve carry the most weight.
4. **Manifestation works as gift.** The GM frames an opening (Lian falls asleep). The player takes it somewhere (the dream). The best manifestations happen when someone puts something down for someone else to pick up.
5. **The yes-and dynamic is more important than character ownership.** Loose ownership with generous offers produced better fiction than strict lanes would have.
6. **The story isn't horror.** The web doesn't threaten the characters. It dismisses their narrative. Every character arc is a version of "are you who you think you are?" The game produced a story about whether identity survives contact with something that doesn't care about identity. The mechanics didn't prescribe this. They created the conditions.
