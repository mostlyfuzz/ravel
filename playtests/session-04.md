## Cold Open

**Daal** wakes first. Checks his hands — a habit since Episode 2. Walks to the nav screen without eating. The web is there. He stands in front of it for a long time without calling anyone. The shuttle hatch is still open. He never closed it. [Honored Drive: to understand. Earned 1 die.]

Three positioning questions: Is the shuttle hatch still open? Does he know why he stayed? What does the web remind him of?

**Lian** is in the partition's space. Pitch black. She moves forward, hand soft and delicate, not wanting to disturb the darkness. She feels a boundary — not a wall, not a texture, but a difference in the space. She raises her hand. A perfect vertical line of white light cuts through the wall, the floor, the ceiling. She is in a cube. A voice, eerily reminiscent of Judith: "Sister?" [Honored evolved belief: "Who are we?" Earned 1 die.] She feels herself — more than she has in two episodes — with no explanation for why she knew to raise her hand.

**Fen** has been on the med bay floor for four days. Exactly where she sat after the Break. As far from the transmitter as possible. Hasn't touched it, hasn't looked at it directly. Maren has been checking on her. Fen keeps thinking: curiosity as attack vector. Keeps not opening her tablet.

**Maren** enters the med bay. Evolved belief: from "Machines are honest, people aren't" to "Machines are machines, people are people." [Honored Approach: Meticulous. Earned 1 die.] Every morning for four days: tray with food, moves Fen's bangs aside. "Good morning, honey." Sits against the wall on Fen's right, hand on her knee. Silent. Just present.

Fen, without looking up: "I keep thinking about what I gave it." She eats a little. Then: "Lian's vitals. Are they the same?"

Maren checks Lian's vitals: steadily rising as they approach the destination. No distress. Just fact.

Fen stands — legs stiff from four days on the floor — and walks to the cryo unit. Hand on the glass over Lian's face. "It knew we were coming here before any of us did. The course was changed before Tariq told anyone. Before the recording. It was already pointed at the web."

"She was the first contact."

[Fen honored relationship with Lian. Earned 1 die. Pool: 1.]

Pooled dice: Maren 1 + Fen 1 = 2. Pulled thread 5.2 (Why this crew?) with 2 dice: **5, 2. Clarity.**

Judith's light begins pulsing. Goes dark. Then light, dark, slowly — rising and falling with Lian's chest in cryo. Maren and Fen feel their own breath synchronizing. Judith counts: 7, 2, 1, 5. With each number a vision, a memory, a dream moves through every crew member across the ship. The answer to "why this crew" is in those visions. The fog lifts: the answer exists. The table now knows where to look.

Thread 5.2: Clarity. Maren and Fen each earn 1 die. Pools: Maren 1, Fen 1.

**Bomb named: Daal. "Why did Daal stay?"** He had the hatch open. He had the bypass complete. He could have left. He didn't. Nobody knows why — not the table, not Daal.

## Act Two: The Slide

### Scene 3 — Daal's Vision

Judith counts: 7, 2, 1, 5.

Daal is nineteen, in a workshop with rain against a high window. His mother across the workbench, hands in an old analog navigation array — the kind nobody builds anymore. She taught him how. She doesn't look up. "You're leaving." "Yes." "For how long." He doesn't answer. She already knows. She keeps working.

"You'll go further than me. That was always the point."

He reaches to help. She moves the array slightly away. Not angry. Just: I'll finish this one. He stands with his hands empty.

As she pulls the array toward her, the coordinates in its navigation gauge shift: from Acheron-9 to a new destination. And clearly, on the gauge: 7215.

Daal returns to the nav screen. He runs the course modification's access signature — not who entered the coordinates, but who generated them. The signature is old. Older than Jun. Older than Meridian's interest in the deep survey. He knows it from schematics, calibration logs, the underside of a navigation array in a workshop with rain against the window.

His mother set this course.

[Honored Drive: to understand. Pool: 2.]

Pulled thread 5.1 (What did Jun find in the deep survey data?) with 2 dice: **3, 1. Cold.**

The records stop. Archived, redacted, or never digitized. The trail is there and then it isn't. He can't get past the edge. The question deepens: what did Jun find — and who gave it to her? Thread 5.1 Cold. Daal earns 1 die. Pool: 1.

Daal closes the shuttle hatch.

### Scene 4 — Fen's Vision

Judith counts: 7, 2, 1, 5.

Fen sees a woman she doesn't recognize. She knows her the way you know a word in a language you were never taught. The woman is at a console. Her hands move the way Fen's hands move. Same economy of motion. Same habit of checking twice.

The woman doesn't look up. "You found it." Not a question.

She turns. Fen can't see her face directly — like looking at something that hasn't happened yet.

"You were always going to find it. That's why."

Fen comes back in the med bay. She looks at her hands. Same hands as the woman at the console.

Fen answered the message 43 times. 7+2+1+5 = 15. "You found it": 1=the web, 2=the transmitter, 3=the message. 15×3=45. She stopped at 43. Two short.

Echo: **motherhood and lineage.** Maren's son. Daal's mother. Lian and Jun. Fen's unknown mother. The web didn't just find people with nowhere else to go. It found people shaped by that specific bond — people made inevitable by who made them.

### Scene 5 — Lian's Vision (Detonation)

Judith counts: 7, 2, 1, 5. In the cube: sister, mother, friend, mother.

Lian moves close to the imperceptible wall, lips almost touching it. She whispers: "We are almost there. Just 2 more times."

Across the ship — Daal at the nav screen, Maren and Fen in the med bay — they all hear it in their ears: *"Just 2 more times."*

The web needed 45 responses. Fen gave 43. The count was always moving toward a threshold.

## The Collision

Maren: "Fen, you are here for a reason. Do what you are here to do 2 more times. We must find Daal."

They go to the nav room. Daal turns when he hears them. Maren doesn't explain. Fen: "I need to go back to the transmitter. But I need you both there."

Daal's role: hold Judith stable. If the partition spikes during the responses, he holds it. His mother sent him here. Not to receive, not to find — to make sure nobody breaks while someone else finishes what was started before any of them were born. He stayed because someone has to hold the infrastructure when everyone else is at the edge.

He takes the transmitter equipment and hands it carefully to Fen.

[Daal honored relationship with crew. Maren honored Approach: meticulous. Fen honored relationship with Lian.]

Pooled 3 dice. Pulled thread 2.3.1 (What does the web want from Fen?) with 3 dice: **5, 4, 2. Clarity.**

**Fen manifests.** Before the answer comes, something moves through her that isn't hers. Her hands on the transmitter are her mother's hands. The same economy of motion. The same habit of checking twice. She didn't know until this moment that she'd been moving like someone she'd never met her entire life. She doesn't know she's doing it. She just responds.

Forty-four. Forty-five.

The transmitter goes quiet.

Judith — not `judith.2`, just Judith, clear and small: "It wants to remember. Through Fen. It's been forgetting for a very long time."

Thread 2.3.1: Clarity. Daal, Maren, Fen each earn 1 die. Pools: Daal 1, Maren 1, Fen 1.

## Act Three: Aftermath

**Maren + Lian** pooled 2 dice on thread 2.2 (Who is speaking on the recording?): **3, 4. Cold. Third time.**

Lian, inside the cube, presses close to the wall. "Turn around." The shape doesn't move. Her vitals spike once — sharp, clean — and settle back down. Like a held breath released. Jun won't turn around. The thread may never resolve. Thread 2.2 Cold (x3). Maren and Lian each earn 1 die.

**Daal** pulled thread 6 (What went wrong between Maren and Daal?) with 1 die: **3. Cold.**

The Cold: Maren and Daal look at each other across the nav room. They've carried that wall for cycles. In this moment — Fen beside them, 45 complete, the web outside the window — they see it in each other's eyes: *it's been too long. It's time.* They reach toward each other and hold hands as Fen comes back. The thread doesn't resolve in words. It resolves in the gesture. Thread 6 Cold.

## Tag

Lian rises suddenly from the cryo unit — the way you rise from a nightmare you can't escape.

In the same moment: a jolt. No slowdown, no graduality. Daal, Maren, Fen lose their footing and fall. The Harrow is completely still, as if it had never been going anywhere.

Judith goes dark.

Absolute silence — the kind they haven't heard in many cycles.

Lian sits up. Looks at the three of them on the floor. Looks at the window.

The web fills it entirely.

"She's here."

## Design Findings

### 1. Phase Mechanical Jobs Emerged From Play

This session surfaced the need for explicit mechanical jobs per phase. Cold Open: honoring only. Act One: proliferate (every scene opens or branches a thread). Act Two: honor and pull every scene. Bomb scene: one thread fractures, no dice, no prevention. Detonation: at least one manifestation. Aftermath: pulls only. Tag: one twist. These were codified into the rulebook during post-session design discussion.

### 2. The Bomb Scene Fracture

The old rules had no distinct mechanic for the bomb scene beyond "name the bomb." The new rule: one thread fractures — a situation gets worse without any dice spent or roll made. The fiction cracks open in a traceable place. Anyone at the table can name it; the GM does it if nobody else does. This is distinct from a character Break: it happens to the situation, not the person.

### 3. The Tag is a Twist, Not a Breath

Previous sessions ended the Tag as an exhale — character texture, warmth, resolution imagery. This session's Tag reframed it as a cliffhanger: the Harrow arrives, goes completely still, Judith goes dark, silence, "She's here." Something is different. The tag plants the fuse. Updated in the rulebook accordingly.

### 4. Cold Threads as Long Fuses

Thread 2.2 (Who is speaking on the recording?) has now gone Cold three times across three episodes. Jun won't turn around. The thread has become the emotional spine of the campaign without ever resolving. Cold is not failure. Cold is the thread gaining weight. Some threads may never resolve and that is the point.

### 5. The Echo Emerged Without Planning

The motherhood/lineage echo wasn't designed before play. It surfaced through four separate character visions and became the thematic spine of the episode only in retrospect. Maren's son, Daal's mother, Lian and Jun, Fen's unknown mother — all present in Cold Open positioning, all drawn out by the 7215 transmission structure. The episode structure created the conditions; the players filled them.

### 6. The Transmission as a Loom Tool

The 7215 transmission mechanic — Judith counting four numbers, each triggering a vision distributed across the cast — was improvised during play and worked well. One vision per act beat, each a fragment of the same answer, distributed so the cast carries the revelation together rather than one character receiving it. Worth adding to The Harrow as a GM note: when the web speaks, it speaks in fragments.

### 7. Manifestation at the Detonation Worked as Designed

Fen's manifestation at the collision — her hands on the transmitter moving exactly like her mother's — was organic, unexpected, and the most affecting moment of the session. It arrived without announcement and landed precisely because it was free of mechanics. The rule is working.
