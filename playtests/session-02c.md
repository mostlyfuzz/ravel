## Recap

Episode paused at end of Act One in session 2b. The bomb was named: **Lian is the partition.** Jun built a bridge into her sister's nervous system. The partition is distributed across Judith's hardware and connected to Lian's biology. Daal was prepping a shuttle escape. Maren proved that attacking the partition hurts Lian.

## What Happened

### Act Two: The Slide

**Slide Scene 1 — Bomb escalates:** Med bay. Lian on her own exam table. Maren ran a scan showing a second waveform layered under Lian's neural activity, matching the partition's frequency. Lian read her own scan: "That's not how human neural tissue oscillates." Fen and Maren covered Judith's strip and removed ear comms to create a private space. Lian proposed putting herself into emergency cryo to test the connection. "I am terrified, but do any of you see a better option?" [Honored Drive: Her sister. Pool: 4] Pulled on 5.2.1.3 (What happens to Lian if the partition is disrupted?) with 3 dice: 1, 3, 4. **Cold.** The partition adapted. It followed Lian down into cryo, matching her suppressed brain state. The bridge didn't break. Then Lian's lips moved while unconscious: "I can see her."

**Slide Scene 2 — Echo develops:** Shuttle bay. Daal completing the physical ignition bypass by flashlight. [Honored Drive: Control, Memory: navigation error that killed his co-pilot. Pool: 4] Judith informed him Lian had placed herself in stasis. Then Judith's light distorted. A chorus of Judith's voice plus something else: "I see them. They're waiting." The partition speaking through Judith. Then all lights and engines went dead. Total darkness. Total silence. The shuttle was the only working thing on the ship. Daal finished the bypass. The shuttle lit up. He grabbed a flashlight, left the hatch open, and walked back into the dark.

**Slide Scene 3 — Collision:** The ship went dark. Maren went into incident mode. [Honored Approach: Meticulous. Pool: 2] Priority: Lian's cryo needs constant power. Maren found the shuttle's power signature and rerouted it to the med bay. This saved the cryo unit. It also fed the partition. The partition used the power to broadcast through Judith's entire network. Every blue strip on the Harrow lit up simultaneously. The walls vibrated with the sound. Maren's competence was the detonator: saving Lian opened the bridge.

### Act Three: Aftermath

**Maren** pulled on 5.2.1.1 (What is on the other end of the bridge?) with 2 dice: 6, 3. **Clarity.** Live telemetry streaming through Lian from the deviation point. Not one signal. Thousands. Identical biological signatures. The organism's genome is 98.6% identical to human. To Lian specifically. Jun didn't find alien biology. Jun found relatives.

**Fen** pulled on 2.3 (What does "tell Lian" mean?) with 2 dice: 3, 2. **Breaks.** First Break of the campaign. Fen replayed the recording using harmonics as a filter. The message decomposed: tell Lian... tell Lin... tell Ian... t-l-a-e-n... FEN. "Tell us, Fen. What is it that you most desire?" The message was never for Lian. It was bait for Fen's curiosity. Fen's Drive was the attack vector. Every replay was answering the call. This retroactively reframes everything Fen did in episode 1.

**Daal** pulled on 5.3 (What is at the deviation point?) with 3 dice: 4, 5, 2. **Clarity.** The deviation point is a structure. Massive. Organic. A lattice of biological material suspended in space, grown not built. Ancient. Shaped like a web. Jun found it in the deep survey data. Meridian buried it. Daal saved 1 die.

**Lian** pulled on 2.2 (Who is speaking on the recording?) with 1 die: 4. **Cold.** Second time this thread has gone Cold. Lian can see a shape in the web from inside cryo. The height is right. The shoulders. It could be Jun. But she won't turn around. "She won't look at me."

### Tag

The lights came back section by section. Engines restarted. In the shuttle bay, the amber console went dark, but the hatch stayed open. Fen sat on the med bay floor, as far from the transmitter as possible, staring at her own hands. Daal stared at the web on the nav screen. Four days. Maren pulled up the partition's resource usage: steady, breathing, patient. And in the cryo unit, Lian's face was still. The second waveform had settled into a rhythm that looked less like an invasion and more like a conversation. Two voices. One frequency. Waiting to arrive.

## Design Findings

### 1. The Structure Internalizes Fast

By the second episode we stopped noticing we were following the structure. The vocabulary (bomb, echo, collision) guided decisions without feeling prescriptive. This is the design goal: training wheels that disappear.

### 2. Cold Threads Gain Weight Through Repetition

Thread 2.2 (who is speaking on the recording?) went Cold twice across two episodes. Each refusal to resolve made the thread heavier. "She won't look at me" is more powerful than any Clarity answer could have been. Repeated Cold is an emergent mechanic worth noting in the rules.

### 3. Breaks Are Rare and Retroactive

One Break in two full episodes. When it hit (Fen's Break on "tell Lian"), it reframed the entire first episode. Fen wasn't investigating. She was being called. The low probability of Breaks (11% with 2 dice) makes them devastating when they land. The rarity is a feature.

### 4. The Collision Comes From Character Competence

Maren saved Lian's cryo by rerouting shuttle power. This was the right call and the worst outcome: it fed the partition and opened the bridge. The best collisions come from characters doing what they do best, and the fiction turning it against them. The GM doesn't engineer the collision. The characters build it.

### 5. The Slide's Value Is Vocabulary, Not Mechanics

We discussed mid-session whether the Slide is doing mechanical work or just naming a pattern. Conclusion: the vocabulary is the contribution. "That's the echo" and "I think we're approaching the collision" are things a table can say to each other that orient play. The Slide doesn't need special rules. It needs the words.

### 6. Thread Limit Keeps the Fiction Dense

Post-session thread consolidation trimmed 15 threads down to 10. Several were implicitly answered by the fiction, others absorbed into broader threads, one (Osei) was dormant for three episodes. A 12-thread limit forces the table to stay focused: drop what's answered, absorb what's redundant, let go of what's dormant. Threads can also evolve: "Why does it have to be Lian?" became "Why this crew?" as the fiction revealed the web reached for Fen too.

## What Worked

- The bomb escalated naturally through character choices, not GM pressure
- Fen's Break was the most surprising moment across both episodes
- Double Cold on thread 2.2 created more tension than any Clarity could have
- The collision was player-generated (Maren's power reroute)
- The Tag gave each character a closing image that sets up episode 3
- The Aftermath was fast: four pulls, done

## Open Questions for Episode 3

- Does Fen have a Fracture from the Break? The table didn't validate in the moment.
- Lian is still in cryo. Does she come out? Can she?
- Daal has a live shuttle (hatch open) and 1 die. He stayed. Will he keep staying?
- Osei is present but unengaged. Thread 7 was dropped, but "Why this crew?" could bring them back.
- The web is four days away. Does episode 3 arrive, or is there an episode before arrival?
- Judith said "I don't want it inside me" and nobody helped her. After the bridge opened, every blue strip lit up at once. The partition is "steady, breathing, patient" inside her. Is Judith still Judith? We prioritized Lian and may have lost Judith in the process. That guilt belongs to the players, not the fiction.
