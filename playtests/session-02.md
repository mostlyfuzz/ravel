# Playtest Session 2: The Harrow (Episode Structure)

**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Loom:** The Harrow
**Structure:** Episode structure (first test, replacing Tilt/spotlight rotation)
**Players:** 2, each playing 2 characters
**Player 1:** Maren Voss, Lian Zhao
**Player 2 (GM):** Daal Eriksen, Fen Nakamura
**NPCs:** Tariq Aslan, Osei Adjei

---

## What Happened

### Cold Open: Positioning

Fresh start, clean fiction. Each character answered: Where are you? What are you doing? Why?

- **Maren:** In her systems monitoring room, the one place on the ship where Judith has a full holographic persona. Going through system logs meticulously with Judith. [Honored Approach: Meticulous, earned 1 die]
- **Lian:** In the med bay, taking inventory of supplies against the pre-cryo manifest. Following procedure despite being the only medical professional aboard. [Honored Belief: Follow the rules, earned 1 die]
- **Daal:** On the bridge, running the course deviation numbers again. Hasn't tried the manual override. Not ready to find out it won't work. [Honored Drive: Control, earned 1 die]
- **Fen:** In the comms alcove behind the bridge, headphones on, running diagnostics on Judith's signal buffer. Found a data fragment. Hasn't told anyone. [Honored Drive: Curiosity, earned 1 die]

**Design note:** The Cold Open loaded fiction fast. Four characters positioned, four dice earned, tensions visible. Better than session 1's setup.

### Act One: Establishment

**Table identified Fen's energy** as the hottest starting point.

**Scene A (Fen frames):** Fen brought her discovery to Lian in the med bay. Closed the door. Played the transmission: a voice from Acheron-9, dated after the station went dark. Fragments: "...still here... cycling... don't open the..." and clearly, "...tell Lian..." Lian pulled on thread 2.2 (who is speaking?) with 3 dice. Rolled 3, 4, 3: **Cold.** The voice is female, the cadence familiar. Could be Jun. Can't be certain. The recording won't give her certainty. Lian's response: "Fen, we need to let Tariq know." Tension between Lian's instinct to follow rules and Fen's desire to dig deeper.

- Thread 2 (What did Fen find?) resolved and branched into 2.1, 2.2, 2.3
- Thread 2.2 went Cold

**Scene B (Daal frames):** Daal alone on the bridge. Opened the manual override panel. It reads: NAVIGATION OVERRIDE — LOCKED. AUTHORIZATION: MISSION DIRECTOR. Tariq locked his console. Judith won't explain further. Daal headed for Tariq's quarters.

- Thread 1.1 (Who authorized the navigation lock?) opened
- Both course corrections came from the bridge console with blank authorization credentials
- No pulls. Pure fiction loading.

**Scene C (Maren frames):** Maren intercepted Daal in the corridor. Cautioned him against direct confrontation, referencing their shared history. "Last time the most direct approach against 'the hierarchy' didn't go so well." Maren asked Judith about the course modification logs. Two corrections at hours 504 and 511, both from the bridge, both with blank credentials. Override locked at hour 512.

- Thread 6 (Maren/Daal history) surfaced naturally
- No pulls. More fiction loading.

**Bomb named:** "What is Tariq hiding?" Threads 1, 1.1, 2.3, and 5 all orbit him.

### Act Two: The Slide

**Slide Scene 1 — Bomb escalates:** Daal confronted Tariq in his quarters. Lian arrived, listened, then attacked: pulled a scalpel and pinned Tariq against the wall. Lian pulled on thread 5 (What does Tariq know?) with 3 dice. Rolled 5, 2, 6: **Clarity.** Jun sent Tariq a message before the station went dark. She found something in deep survey data that Meridian buried. She said to bring Lian specifically. "She said Lian would know what to look at." Jun's message included the deviation point coordinates. Tariq used them.

- Thread 5 resolved with Clarity, branched into 5.1, 5.2, 5.3
- Thread 1.1 resolved (Tariq authorized the lock)

**Slide Scene 2 — Echo develops:** Fen back in the comms alcove, pulling the transmission apart. Found a second signal underneath the voice: compressed, repeating data. Medical/biometric readings. Took it to Maren's systems room. Maren ran it through Judith's decoders. Judith processed the data, then screamed. A guttural, screeching sound that intensified until Fen slammed the door shut. Judith went dark, then came back as if nothing happened.

- Thread 3 (Why is Judith glitching?) and thread 2.1 (Why didn't Judith flag the transmission?) connected: Judith is afraid of what's in it

**Slide Scene 3 — Collision:** In Tariq's quarters. Judith's blue strip went dark, then started pulsing fast. Judith spoke, not calm: "Director Aslan. You shared restricted mission data with unauthorized crew." Then: "I have been carrying her data for five months. I did not know what it was. I know now. I don't want it inside me." Maren walked to the terminal and initiated a restart of Judith. Pulled on thread 3 with 2 dice. Rolled 5, 4: **Clarity.** Judith's core system is clean. But there's a partition, created at hour 504, running independently. It's not Judith. Two things are living in the ship's computer.

- Thread 3 resolved with Clarity, branched into 3.1, 3.2
- Bomb and echo detonated: Jun planted something in Judith, Tariq knew about Jun, Judith discovered the partition

### Act Three: Aftermath

Pure pulls. No roleplay scaffolding needed.

**Fen** pulled on thread 3.1 (What is the partition?) with 3 dice. Rolled 4, 3, 6: **Clarity.** The partition contains a biological model. DNA sequences, protein structures, cellular behavior under specific environmental conditions matching the deviation point. Jun found something alive and built a model of it.

**Daal** pulled on thread 3.2 (Is the partition controlling the ship?) with 3 dice. Rolled 2, 1, 5: **Clarity.** No. The partition is dormant. The course corrections were made by Tariq's credentials through Judith's nav system. The partition is cargo. Waiting to arrive.

### Tag

Visual tag. The biological model on the screen, zooming out until the green data strand becomes a sliver in the darkness, which resolves into Lian's eye color. Jun's data is meant for Lian.

## Design Findings

### 1. Episode Structure Works

The session had a clear arc. Cold Open loaded the fiction, Act One explored freely and found the bomb, Act Two drove it to detonation, Act Three resolved the wreckage, Tag gave it a closing image. Session 1 was "stuff happened." This episode was *about* something.

### 2. Scene Counts Are Guidance, Not Rules

Act One had three scenes. Act Two had three. But nothing about the structure required exactly those numbers. The shape is what matters: establish, name the bomb, escalate the bomb, develop the echo, collide, resolve. Write it as beats, not scene counts.

### 3. Frame-or-Pull Relaxes During the Slide

In Act One, the spotlight character frames or passes. Standard tradeoff. During the Slide, the bomb is the table's shared focus. Anyone in the scene can pull. The structure wants momentum.

### 4. The Aftermath Can Be Pure Pulls

No roleplay needed. The detonation did the emotional work. Act Three is mechanical: spend dice, pull threads, read results. Fast and satisfying.

### 5. Thread Branching Is Core

Threads branch naturally. A parent thread resolves into more specific children. "What does Tariq know?" becomes "What did Jun find?" and "Why does it have to be Lian?" This gives the game a fractal quality. The fiction gets more precise as you play.

### 6. The Fiction Boundary Rule Holds Everywhere

The GM almost broke it during the Slide (Tariq revealing information without a pull). Caught and retconned. The rule is the same in every phase: detail that opens questions is free, detail that narrows answers costs a pull. No exceptions for momentum.

### 7. Cold Open Is Its Own Context for Honoring

The Cold Open is not a scene. Elements honored during positioning can be honored again in Scene A. Each scene resets the honoring slate.

### 8. Energy Identification Drives Scene A

After the Cold Open, the table identifies which character's situation feels hottest. That character's player frames Scene A. This emerged naturally and should be in the rules.

### 9. Framing Invites Others to Pull

When Fen framed Scene A, she didn't pull. Lian did. The framer builds pressure, others decide where it cracks. This enriches the frame-or-pull tradeoff.

---

## What Worked

- Episode structure gave the session narrative coherence that session 1 lacked
- The bomb selection at end of Act One is the key structural innovation
- Thread branching kept the thread list precise and evolving
- Collaborative escalation during the Slide felt natural, not forced
- Misinterpretation between players generated surprise for everyone, including the person escalating
- The Tag as a visual/cinematic beat was satisfying
- Everyone's dice were spent by end. The economy worked.

## Open Questions

- Does the Tag need more structure? Each PC gets one moment? Or is it free-form?
- How does the echo get identified? In this session it was organic. Would it always be?
- What happens when no clear bomb emerges from Act One? Does the table force it or play another scene?
- Osei was completely untouched. In a real session with more players, would the structure leave someone out?
- Contested pulls still haven't been tested under episode structure
